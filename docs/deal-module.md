Now that we have understood **Leads** and **Lead Activities**, let us move forward to another important component of the **CRM system** — the **Deals Module**.

The **Deals Module** helps **sales teams** manage potential business opportunities, track revenue, monitor deal progress, and manage the complete **sales pipeline** from qualification to closure.

---

### What is a Deal?

A **Deal** represents a potential **business opportunity** that is actively being pursued with a customer or organization.

Once a **lead** shows genuine interest and enters the **sales process**, it can be converted into a **deal**.

**Deals** help organizations:

- **Track ongoing sales opportunities**
- **Monitor revenue potential**
- **Manage negotiations**
- **Track pipeline stages**
- **Forecast business revenue**
- **Improve sales visibility**

---

### Importance of Deals Module

The **Deals Module** is important because it helps organizations:

- **Track business opportunities** systematically
- **Monitor deal progress** in real time
- **Improve sales forecasting**
- **Manage customer negotiations**
- **Organize sales pipelines**
- **Improve collaboration** between sales teams
- **Increase deal conversion** rates

Without proper **deal management**, organizations may lose visibility into active opportunities and **revenue tracking**.

---

## Deal Lifecycle

A deal typically moves through multiple stages before it is won or lost.

### Common Deal Stages

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| Qualification | Initial validation of the opportunity |
| Value Proposition | Understanding customer requirements and offering solutions |
| Proposal / Pricing | Sharing quotations or pricing details |
| Negotiation / Review | Negotiating terms and finalizing discussions |
| Closed Won | Deal successfully completed |
| Closed Lost | Opportunity lost or cancelled |

---

## Deal Pipeline Management

A Pipeline in the Deals Module is a structured sequence of stages that represents the journey of a deal from the initial opportunity stage to final closure.

It helps sales teams visually track where each deal currently stands in the sales process.

### Purpose of a Pipeline

Pipelines help organizations:

- Organize sales opportunities
- Track deal progress
- Monitor sales stages
- Forecast revenue
- Identify stuck deals
- Improve sales process visibility

### How a Pipeline Works

Each deal moves through different stages inside a pipeline.

As the sales process progresses, the deal is moved from one stage to another.

---

 **Example of a Sales Pipeline**

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| Qualification | Initial evaluation of the opportunity |
| Value Proposition | Understanding customer requirements |
| Proposal / Pricing | Sharing pricing or quotation |
| Negotiation | Discussing terms and finalizing discussions |
| Closed Won | Deal successfully completed |
| Closed Lost | Opportunity lost or cancelled |

### Pipeline Visualization

The CRM visually displays the pipeline using a stage progression tracker.

**Example**

```text
Qualification → Proposal → Negotiation → Closed Won
```

This helps users quickly understand:

- Current deal status
- Next steps
- Probability of closure

### Multiple Pipelines

Organizations can maintain multiple pipelines for different business processes.

**Examples**

- Sales Pipeline
- Enterprise Sales Pipeline
- Renewal Pipeline
- Partner Pipeline

This helps teams manage different types of opportunities separately.

---

### Relationship Between Pipeline and Stage

Each pipeline contains predefined stages.

When creating a deal:

- Users first select a pipeline
- Then select a stage belonging to that pipeline

The CRM validates that the selected stage belongs to the selected pipeline.

### Deal Activities

#### Available Deal Activity Tabs

| Activity Tab | Purpose |

| Tasks | Manage follow-up tasks |
